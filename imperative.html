Said & Said to– change into commanded,ordered,requested,advised, Conjunction‘to’is used in indirect speech.

 For example:
a)Direct:Teacher said to him,“Do not waste time.”
Indirect:Teacher advised him not to waste time.
(‘Do not’changes into ‘not to’)


b)Direct:The Boss said to him’“Get out of the room.”
Indirect:The Boss ordered him to get out of the room.


c)Direct:The oldwoman said to her,“Please help me."
Indirect:The oldwoman requested her to help her.
(‘please’is replaced by‘requested’)


‘Let’Sentences:
Said and said to changes into suggested and proposed
Conjunction‘that’and‘should’is used in indirect speech


For example:
a)Direct:He said to me,“Let us go for a picnic"
Indirect:He suggested me that we should go for a picnic.
